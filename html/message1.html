A Message to the First Year Pharm D student

  1. Hurray! it's Pharm D!
  1. Our lives revolve around so many things and one of the most important is pharmacy, whether we all realize it or not. When we understand this prestigious programme we have decided to pursue, we see how life itself would be impossible without it, how the quality of our lives depends upon it, and in the health system, how pharmacy leaps out at us from every direction. That’s why we can envision students enthusiastically exclaiming “It’s PharmD!” when, perhaps, they explain to a friend or family member how one central theme—pharmacy— pervades our existence.The pharmacy profession and the role of pharmacists in the modern healthcare systems have evolved quite rapidly over the last couple of decades. The services that pharmacists provide are expanding with the introduction of supplementary prescribing, provision of health checks, patient counselling and many others.

    The main ethos of pharmacy profession is now as much about keeping people healthy as treating them when they are not well. The modern pharmacy profession is shifting away from a product focus and towards a patient focus. To cope with these changes, and to meet the demand of the modern pharmacy profession, the pharmacy curriculum, especially in the developed world, has evolved signiﬁcantly. In the western countries, almost all registered pharmacists are employed by the community and hospital pharmacies. As a consequence, the practice, law, management, care, prescribing science and clinical aspects of pharmacy have become the main components of pharmacy curriculum. We want to help students experience the excitement of seeing the world through a pharmaceutical lens, and how the unifying and simplifying nature of pharmacy helps make many things in health system comprehensible. Our software package makes it possible for students to learn and get quick access to majority but not all of the materials needed for survival in pharmacy school especially the first year and to save them from ignorance as they are new in the faculty and have little knowledge about what it takes to make it successfully through the first year.

    Our software package, we believe, would help students obtain information relevant to the courses taught in the first and second semesters of year 1. It contains some of the lecture powerpoints which are not exhaustive enough for a better preparation for tests and exams, and as well getting in depth understanding of the course, and hence, some recommended textbooks are also included in this package. Sample questions to assist students in studying are also included but we would advise that they are only to guide. This is the vision we want to put across, getting access to valuable information geared towards the improvement in academic work of all and sundry. Let us hasten to add that, relying only on this material without attending lectures and laboratory sessions, or copying part of this material directly for assignments and laboratory reports is like doing no good to oneself. Our advice is that, use this material in conjunction with the understanding obtained from lectures and lab sessions. We apologise that this package is in the software form and hence becomes only accessible to students with personal computers or laptops.

    The beauty in pharmacy school is getting along smoothly with the academic requirements every semester and we are glad we intend to assist in that by this package. After all, it’s pahrmD! We are guided by these goals, and we want the welfare of our dear colleague first year students. May the good Lord bless us all and see us through this journey successfully.
