  1. Name: Vera Asare
    Class: Pharm D2


    WHY I CHOSE TO READ DOCTOR OF PHARMACY

    Actually, the truth is that my parents asked my to choose pharmacy but I guess I am enjoying it now.

    Challenges in first year

    Hmmm!!! There were too many lab reports and very small time for learning, and lectures too were tiresome.

    How I overcame these challenges

    As the adge goes: "the first step towards a soving a problem is to identify what the problem is", I realized that there was not enough time so I improvised and I learned how to manage my time well.

    My Advice to the First Years

    First things should always be first if you want to succeed. In pharmacy, the first thing you have to consider before any other thing is God, so make sure you put God first no matter the circumstances: when you are about to study, write a quiz or start a lecture, remember God.


  1. Name: Lawrencia Gyimah Akoma
    Class: Pharm D2


    WHY I CHOSE TO READ DOCTOR OF PHARMACY

    Looking at the help pharmacists do offer to people, the various job opportunities available in the pharmacy world, and the kind of places I can go with a Doctor of Pharmacy degree, I dare not chose any programme to study over this one.


    Challenges in first year

    I know am not the only one who faced this problem because that is the reality of our course; the syllabus is packed and the lab reports just weighs me down.

    How I overcame these challenges

    I had to do everything possible to fit into the system just as all the others who have made it through first year. It was a game of survival of the fittest and one way to keepup with the heat was to learn, so I devoted much time to learning.

    My Advice to the First Years

    The advice that I have for you is not anything knew but the same old story that you have been hearing only that this thing round you have to stick to it or forget the pharmacy degree. Take your studies seriously because first year especially the first semester will determine your fate in this faculty and the university as a whole.

  1. Name: Abigail Adiyiah
    Class: Pharm D3


    WHY I CHOSE TO READ DOCTOR OF PHARMACY

    Because I have always had the passion to be a pharmacist and I saw this opportunity as a dream come true.

    Challenges in first year

    First year in pharmacy is very challenging, indeed one of the most challenging years in the faculty. Things were in a hurry and pretty soon I had lots of handouts pilling up. With lots of things to read everyday and less time to play, I just couldn't put up with the pressure until I started adopting new measures.

    How I overcame these challenges

    In order to survive the pressure I re-oriented my self and joined study groups for discussions everyday after school. Occassionally I let friends who understood a course better than I did help me in case I had difficulties, before the next lecture. I also made more time for studies and things finally normalised for me.

    My Advice to the First Years

    The beginning of everything is not easy but it becomes easy when you find the secret ingredient. Make friends with some senior colleagues who can keep you well informed on the dos and the don'ts of the faculty and tell you some of the ways to adapt to this stressful system. Make sure you are part of an effective study group and also seek help from your colleagues if need be.


  1. Name: Nancy Affotey Tawiah
    Class: Pharm D3


    WHY I CHOSE TO READ DOCTOR OF PHARMACY

    I must confess, it's by chance that I got into pharmacy school

    Challenges in first year

    Stress!! This is one thing that no pharmacy student is ever spared from. It took a lot of efforts to learn to cope with the stress of first in the faculty of Pharmacy and pharmaceutical Sciences.

    How I overcame these challenges

    What else could I have done, I just learnt to adapt to the stress by reading my handouts after being taught to avoid them from pilling up and also trying to finish my lab reports within the shortest possible time.
